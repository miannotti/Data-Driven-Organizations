# Claims

Understanding how customer characteristics relate to differences in observed costs.

---

## Overview

This case explores how different characteristics are associated with variation in observed costs.

The dataset includes 1,338 individuals, with demographic and behavioral variables such as age, gender, BMI, number of dependents, smoking status, and region.

For each individual, total charges are observed.

---

## Objective

The goal is not only to describe the data, but to understand:

- Why some customers generate higher costs  
- Which factors are associated with those differences  
- How multiple characteristics combine to shape outcomes  

---

## Business Context

This is a cost and risk problem:

- Costs are not evenly distributed  
- Customers do not represent the same level of risk  
- Outcomes are shaped by multiple interacting factors  

---

## Variables

| Variable   | Description |
|------------|------------|
| age        | Age of the individual |
| sex        | Gender (male, female) |
| bmi        | Body Mass Index, proxy for health condition |
| children   | Number of dependents covered |
| smoker     | Smoking status (yes, no) |
| region     | Geographic area (northeast, northwest, southeast, southwest) |
| charges    | Total charges associated with the individual |

---

## Why it matters

Understanding these patterns can support:

- Pricing decisions  
- Risk segmentation  
- Customer management  

---

## Key idea

Costs are not driven by a single factor, but by the interaction of multiple variables.
